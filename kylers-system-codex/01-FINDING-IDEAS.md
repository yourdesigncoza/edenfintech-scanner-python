# Step 1: Finding Ideas

## Core Method

Primary workflow is top-down:
1. Identify underperforming sectors/geographies with upcoming catalysts.
2. Keep only sectors you understand and that are not in secular decline.
3. Build a shortlist of companies with potential to double+ in 2-3 years.
4. Group by theme so comparison is done against true peers.

Secondary sources:
- Substack writers
- Podcasts and YouTube
- News flow that reveals neglected themes
- Broken-chart scans (60-80%+ drawdowns)

## Initial Selection Rules

Keep a name only if all are true:
- Business model is understandable in plain language
- Industry has at least GDP-like growth or cyclical mean-reversion potential
- There is at least one credible catalyst class (macro, operational, regulatory, management, demand)

## Exclusion Zones (Default Pass)

From wiki history and repeated comments, common avoid zones:
- Airlines, banks, biotech, most insurers
- Pure AI/speculative software pockets
- Car manufacturing OEMs (not necessarily suppliers)
- Commodity miners, marine freight, tobacco, restaurants, textiles, trading firms

Exclusion is not absolute. It means "extraordinary proof required".

## Codex Extrapolation

Track two lists, not one:
- `Active Theme List`: names currently under active work
- `Cold Opportunity List`: interesting, but missing catalyst timing

A stock can be cheap and still belong only in Cold until trigger conditions are defined.
